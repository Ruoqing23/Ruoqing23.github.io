About Team TRIO

Team TRIO consists of three members, led by a supervisor, focusing on the research and development of the Water Waste Collection Robot Project. With our respective professional skills, we work together to solve water waste pollution issues.

Our Mission

Develop advanced water waste collection robots to efficiently clean water waste, protect aquatic ecosystems, and improve water environment quality.

Our Vision

Become a leader in the field of water waste cleaning, helping water bodies around the world regain clarity and beauty.

Our Values

Innovation, collaboration, focus, and responsibility are the values we uphold, which run through every stage of the project.

Project Background

By 2025, marine plastic pollution has reached a critical level, with over 11 million tons of plastic waste entering the oceans annually. This causes severe damage to ecosystems and threatens both marine life and human health. Traditional marine waste collection methods rely heavily on manual labor, which is inefficient, unsafe, and limited by dynamic environmental conditions such as waves and currents.

To address these challenges, Team TRIO is dedicated to developing an autonomous robotic boat capable of intelligently detecting and collecting floating waste. Our robotic boat integrates advanced environmental sensing systems, real-time path planning, and efficient collection mechanisms, enabling safe operation in open water areas.

Project Progress

The development journey of the Water Waste Collection Robot Project, from conceptualization to prototype implementation.

Phase 1

Market Research & Needs Analysis

Research existing water waste cleaning equipment, analyze market needs and technical gaps, and determine the core functions and performance indicators of the robot.

Completion Date: March 2025
Phase 2

Concept Design & Plan Development

Conduct conceptual design based on research results, determine the overall structure, power system and collection mechanism of the robot, and formulate detailed technical plans.

Completion Date: April 2025
Phase 3

3D Modeling & Simulation Analysis

Establish a 3D model of the robot, conduct structural strength analysis and motion simulation, optimize the design scheme, and ensure the feasibility and stability of the robot.

Completion Date: May 2025
Phase 4

Hardware Selection & Construction

Select and purchase core components, build the robot's hardware platform, including hull, power system, sensors and collection mechanism.

Completion Date: June 2025
Phase 5

Software System Development

Develop the robot's control system software, implement autonomous navigation, obstacle avoidance, waste recognition and collection functions, and conduct unit testing.

Completion Date: July 2025
Phase 6

System Integration & Test Optimization

Perform software and hardware system integration, test in actual water environments, collect data and optimize to improve robot performance and stability.

Completion Date: August 2025
